The JABRAN Method™ · Applied

How we isolate a production decline

When output falls, the visible symptom is rarely the cause. A drop recorded in one department is often produced upstream — in planning handoffs, material availability, changeover practice, or maintenance timing. Our audit works the problem in sequence: observe the operation as it actually runs, quantify where the losses occur, trace them to their source, and rank the fixes by commercial impact. No finding is reported that we haven't verified on the floor.

01 · Discover

Walk the line end-to-end with the people who run it. Map the real process — not the SOP version.

02 · Diagnose

Measure where time, capacity, and material are actually lost, and trace each loss to its root cause.

03 · Design

Sequence corrective actions by impact and feasibility for this facility's scale and budget.

04 · Deliver

Implement against agreed milestones, with findings categorised by severity and owner.

05 · Develop

Leave a reporting and KPI discipline the plant team can run without us.

Audit Scope Matrix

What a full plant audit examines

Eight operational domains, reviewed on-site. Each domain is assessed against what we observe, what we measure, and what the findings reveal about lost output.

No. Domain What we examine What it typically reveals
01 Production Planning & Flow Scheduling logic, order sequencing, handoffs between planning and the floor, WIP levels between stations. Idle capacity created by planning gaps rather than machine limits.
02 Machine Utilisation & Downtime Rated vs. actual output per line, changeover duration, downtime logging accuracy, minor-stoppage patterns. Equipment running well below rated capacity for reasons no one has quantified.
03 Material Movement & Layout Travel distances, staging areas, double-handling, congestion points, layout vs. process sequence. Labour hours consumed moving material instead of converting it.
04 Inventory & Stores Control Raw material availability at point of use, stock accuracy, slow-moving and obsolete stock, issue discipline. Line stoppages caused by material shortages the system said didn't exist.
05 Quality Control Process Inspection points and timing, rejection and rework handling, root-cause discipline on recurring defects. Quality issues caught too late — after value has already been added to defective product.
06 Maintenance Practice Preventive vs. reactive ratio, breakdown history by asset, spares availability, maintenance-production coordination. Unplanned downtime concentrated in a small number of poorly maintained assets.
07 Packing & Dispatch End-of-line balance against upstream output, manning levels vs. throughput, dispatch documentation flow. A bottleneck at the end of the line masking healthy capacity upstream — or vice versa.
08 Reporting & KPIs What is measured, how honestly it's recorded, and whether the numbers reach people who can act on them. Losses that persist simply because nobody's report makes them visible.

Every finding in the final report is categorised by severity, evidenced from on-site observation, and paired with a practical corrective action. We do not report benchmark figures we haven't measured in your facility.
