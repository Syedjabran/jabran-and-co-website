The Sourcing Workflow

From requirement to fulfilment — eight controlled stages

Sourcing fails at handoffs — an unwritten specification, an unvetted supplier, an inspection that happens after the goods ship. Our process closes each gap in sequence. Nothing moves to the next stage until the previous one is documented and agreed in writing.

01

Requirement Definition

Written specification, quantities, quality criteria, and commercial terms agreed with the client before any supplier is approached.
02

Market & Supplier Discovery

Candidate suppliers identified across our sourcing markets against the written specification — not from a generic directory.
03

Supplier Screening & Qualification

Capability, capacity, compliance, and track record verified before a supplier is shortlisted.
04

Sampling & Specification Lock

Samples evaluated against the agreed criteria; the approved sample becomes the contractual quality reference.
05

Commercial Negotiation

Pricing, payment terms, lead times, and liability negotiated and documented on the client's behalf.
06

Order & Production Monitoring

Purchase order placed; production progress tracked against milestones, with issues raised while they can still be fixed.
07

Inspection & Quality Verification

Goods inspected against the locked specification and approved sample before dispatch — not after arrival.
08

Logistics, Clearance & Fulfilment

Freight coordination, customs documentation, and delivery managed through to receipt — one accountable point of contact throughout.

Industrial Raw Materials & Chemicals
